A "Foodie" Website

What is the sweetest food you can think of? Some people may think of cupcakes, ice cream, muffins, and pancakes. But personally, if you were to ask me what the sweetest food of all is, I'd say cookies!
There is a wide range of these sweet snacks all over the globe. Here, we'll talk about some of the most notable ones of the bunch.

Recognizable:

  1. Chocolate Chip: Who hasn't heard of this type of cookie? Its what mainly comes to mind when thinking of the concept, and is one of the only ones I am personally most familiar with. Simply the idea of a piece of dough stuffed with small pieces of chocolate is enough to get mouths watering!
  2. Macarons: Derived from the french word maccherone (fine dough), these colorful cookies got their start in Italy in 1533, without the colors that is. It is believed that they were first invented by Pierre Desfontaines for the wedding of King Henry II of France, where he filled the cookies with ganache and stuck them together to create the very cookies we know today.
  3. Alfajores: First created in Argentina in the 8th century, these sandwich cookies are made of two crumbly sweet cookies on the outside, and stuffed with milk jam (dulce de leche). For an added touch, a layer of chocolate or confectioners' sugar can be added. The name comes from the arabic word for stuffed/filled, al-hasú. As time went on, the tradition had expanded from Spain to Latin America, where they are a celebrated tradition!

Popular

  1. Oreo: One of the more popular cookies, outside of the ones mentioned above, Oreo cookies were introduced by Nabisco on March 6, 1912. Mass produced since then, it takes almost 59 minutes to get one cookie made, and more than 123,000 tons are made per year! These are some of the most popular cookies in the world, and despite humble beginings, its easy to see why!
  2. Gingerbread: A major staple in the holidays, these cookies where first invented in Germany between the 16th and 18th centuries, yet the idea that brought them to the way they are today came from Britain. To clarify, it was the idea of Queen Elizabeth I to decorate gingerbread in the form of houses and people. It sprung after some were made resembling the high ranking dignitaries in her court.
  3. Fortune: Have you ever head a chinese meal and found this inside? With how promenant these are in chinese restaurants, you would think that these cookies originated from China. Shockingly, the legendary Fortune Cookie gained its start in Kyoto, Japan. Here, they went by the name of "Japanese Cracker." It is believed that the cracker came to America thanks to Japanese immigrants, who later found work in factories thanks to the Chinese Exclusion Act. However, may immigrants couldn't open Japanese restaurants, as Chinese restaurants, and the food they sereved began to become more popular, and fortune cookies began to sneek their way into these restaurants at the same time. Although, the final nail in the coffin was the Pearl Harbor bombings. With the attack, Executive Order 9066 had lead to the closure of many Japanese owned buisnesses, leaving Chinese restaurants as the only places in America with fortune cookies. And so, it has remained that way for decades, and the fortune cookie still remains one of the most popular treats of all time!

Obscure:

  1. Tareco: Originating in Pernambuco, Brazil, these are hard biscuts consisting of wheat/corn flour, eggs, vanilla, and sugar. Despite their obscue and so-so ratings these cookies have gained popularity in Brazil. So much so, that Brazilian singer Flávio José titled one of his many songs as Tareco e Mariola(Tareco and Mariola), based on the cookie's name!
  2. Fekka: Often referred to as the Moroccan biscotti, these are Moroccan, twice-baked cookies that can be either sweet or savory. These are offten prepared with shortbread or yeasted dough, enriched with orange blossom water, aniseed, or citrus zest, alongside toasted nuts and dried fruit(almonds, walnuts, raisins, pistachios, sesame seeds). They are most commonly seen being served with tea or coffie.
  3. Triggel: Literally one of, of not THE lowest rated cookie in the world, Triggels are Swiss Christmas cookies made with flour, honey, and sugar. The iconic part of these treats is their images engraved at their tops. They decpict Zürich Canton, romance, and Biblical stories, and are different every time. Their name is derived from torggeln, reffering to stirring thick dough. The first written mention of Triggels is back in 1461 in witch court trial documents. They started gaining more traction across the 15th and 16th centuries, and by 1840, only city bakers had the right to bake them. Eventually, all bakers could create these cookies, and the festive treats pop up every now and again, from family to family.
I wonder if this website made you hungry! Perhaps you can take a bite out of one of these one day to satisfy your cravings! So, the next time your at the store or bakery, keep and eye put for these cookies, and try them sometime!
